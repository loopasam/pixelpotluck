Pumpkin Miso Soup

Pumpkin Miso Soup is a delightful fusion of traditional Japanese flavors with the creamy, sweet notes of pumpkin. It's a comforting, nourishing soup that's perfect for chilly days.

⏳ Planning needed: No

🍽️ Servings: 4 servings

🧺 Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil 🏺
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped 🧅
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced 🧄
  • 6 grams fresh ginger, grated (1 tablespoon) 💛
  • 1 liter vegetable broth (4 cups) 🌱
  • 400 to 450 grams of pumpkin puree (2 cups), from a can or home-made 🎃
  • 50 grams white miso paste (3 tablepoons) 🥄
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce 🍶
  • Salt and pepper, to taste 🧂🌑
  • Sliced green onions or nori strips, for garnish (optional) 🟢

🗒️ Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat toasted sesame oil over medium heat 🏺. Add chopped onion 🧅, minced garlic 🧄, and grated ginger 💛. Sauté until the onion 🧅 is translucent, for about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in pumpkin puree 🎃 and vegetable broth 🌱. Bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Add soy sauce 🍶 and miso paste 🥄 into the pot. Be careful not to boil the soup after adding miso to preserve its flavor and probiotic qualities.
  4. Blend the soup with an immersion blender until smooth.
  5. Let the soup simmer for about 20 minutes. Season with salt 🧂 and pepper 🌑 to taste.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with sliced green onions 🟢 or nori strips 🟢 if desired.

✏️ Notes

  • Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 weeks.
  • Use butternut squash as a substitute for pumpkin.
  • For a creamier texture, add a splash of coconut milk.
  • Keeps well in the freezer.
